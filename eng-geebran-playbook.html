Morning Briefing

15 minutes.
Every morning.

This is your most powerful leadership tool. A structured pre-shift briefing — hotel standard (Marriott/Four Seasons protocol adapted for tour operations).

Briefing structure (15–20 min)
1
Appearance check (2 min)Uniform, shoes, badge, hair, hygiene. Walk the line. No exceptions.
2
Energy check-in (2 min)Quick pulse: how is everyone? Any issues to flag? Builds trust.
3
Day focus — one specific goal (3 min)E.g., "Today: ask every guest for a review at snorkel point." One thing only.
4
Yesterday debrief — one case (3 min)One positive story + one thing to improve. Real, specific, brief.
5
Logistics (3 min)Today's assignments, any special guests, dietary notes, weather context
6
Close — energy send-off (2 min)Short call-to-action. "Let's go get 5-star reviews. Have a great day."

Buddy Check System
Before departure, every guide checks their partner:

Uniform ✓ · Badge ✓ · Hair ✓ · Shoes ✓ · Energy ✓

Pairs rotate weekly. System suggests pairings. Takes 60 seconds — builds accountability culture without you policing every person.
Quarterly hospitality sessions
1× per quarter: 90-min team session on guest experience topics. Role-play scenarios. Stories from reviews. Celebrate wins. You facilitate — or bring in a hotel trainer. Content can come from actual guest reviews.
